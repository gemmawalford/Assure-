3. Talk Tracks & Demo Flow

Suggested call structure (30–45 mins)

  1. 3–5 mins: Rapport & agenda
  2. 10–15 mins: Discovery & qualification
  3. 15–20 mins: Tailored demo
  4. 5 mins: Next steps and timeline

Opening talk track

“Thanks for taking the time today. I’d love to quickly understand how you’re handling [problem area] now, share how other customers are approaching it, and then show you how New Product Name could fit into your world.”

Demo storyline

  1. Start: Show the main dashboard / “day in the life”.
  2. Middle: Walk through 1–2 key workflows tied to their pain.
  3. End: Show reporting / ROI or result of the workflow.
Tip: Always tie each feature back to a business outcome, not just “look what this screen does”.

4. Common Objections & Responses

“It’s too expensive.”

Reframe: Anchor on ROI, not price.

“Totally understand budget is tight. Most customers justify this by looking at the cost of [manual process / risk / delays]. Typically we see [X%] reduction in [cost/time] within [Y months]. Would it help to build a simple ROI case together?”

“We’re too busy to implement right now.”

Reframe: Emphasise low lift and support.

“That’s exactly why we simplified onboarding. Our team does most of the heavy lifting, and typical customers go live in [time frame]. Could we walk through what a lightweight rollout might look like for you?”

“We already use another tool.”

Reframe: Differentiate, don’t attack.

“That makes sense, and we integrate well with many existing tools. Where we tend to add value is [key differentiator]. Can I ask what you like / don’t like about your current setup?”
